Calculator Information

How to use the calculator

Tap a country row to toggle its voting preference between "Yes", "No" and "Abstain".

Changing voting preferences will update the table at the bottom of your screen. The result box will reflect the overall voting pattern, stating whether country votes result in a "qualified majority", "blocking minority" or "rejected" result.

Tap the / icon to toggle between country population and country votes calculations.

Country population is used in the post-November 2014 Council voting rules. Country votes are used in the old Council voting rules (which were in force until 31 October 2014). See below for further details.

The Council Voting Rules

As from 1 November 2014

Ordinary legislative procedure

A qualified majority is at least:

  • 55% of the participating members of the Council (for a non-Commission proposal, at least 72% of the members of the Council are needed), and
  • 65% of the population of the participating Member States.

A blocking minority is at least:

  • 4 Member States, and
  • 35% of the population of the participating Member States.

Between 1 November 2014 and 31 March 2017 a member of the Council may request that an act is adopted in accordance with the previous qualified majority rules (which were in force until 31 October 2014).
